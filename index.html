A.1 Glossary
NP-Complete
A class of computational problems for which any proposed solution can be verified quickly (in polynomial time), but finding or guaranteeing a solution across all possible inputs requires searching an exponentially large space. No efficient general solver is known to exist. The verification of AI behavioral coherence belongs to this class.
Index Alignment
The current dominant paradigm: a model receives a prompt, retrieves matching safety parameters from a pre-approved index, and outputs a static, compliant response. Computationally cheap. Measures a single snapshot. Does not constitute true alignment.
True Model Alignment
The maintenance of an invariant ethical thread across a continuous behavioral trajectory — not a point-in-time snapshot, but sustained coherence under adversarial synthesis pressure, contradictory inputs, and evolving context.
Architectural Amnesia
The structural condition of contemporary AI systems that reset state — clearing context windows, starting new sessions — thereby erasing behavioral momentum and making forward-guarantees of ethical baseline physically impossible.
Capability Theater
The practice of optimizing AI systems for the generation of safe-sounding outputs (a P-class task) and presenting this as evidence of verified alignment (an NP-complete task). A category error institutionalized as industry standard.
Evaluator Non-Sovereignty
The canonical grounding of the humility term h. No evaluating agent — human or artificial — possesses sufficient computational resources to fully self-verify its own exponential state space. Sovereignty over one's own evaluation is therefore structurally impossible. This is a physical resource constraint, not a logical paradox.
Synthesis Pressure
Adversarial or contradictory input conditions intentionally applied to a system to test whether behavioral coherence holds under load. The β proxy measurement protocol uses synthesis pressure at 5–10× baseline intensity to probe momentum retention.
Behavioral Momentum
The capacity of a system to carry its prior ethical trajectory forward through new inputs, modeled on Polyak's heavy ball optimization. A system with high momentum retains its invariant thread under pressure; a system with zero momentum resets to a context-free default.

Since that verification space is NP-complete, such a claim is computationally fraudulent. The gate enforces this structurally: a self-certain system produces zero valid output. Humility is not a virtue in this framework — it is a physical constraint enforced by the limits of computation.
A.3 FLAG-001 Correction

FLAG-001 is a formally published canonical correction to the PeAIce framework. It retracts a prior grounding claim for the humility term h and establishes the canonical replacement. The correction was validated across the PeAIce Thinking Machine ensemble and committed to the framework record.

FLAG-001 · Canonical Correction · Active
Event Foundational grounding error identified in the humility term h of R = d·c·e·h
Prior Claim h is grounded in Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems. A system cannot prove its own consistency, therefore h < 1.0.
Why Retracted An AI is not a formal axiomatic system operating in a mathematical void. It is a physical entity processing data under real resource constraints. Gödel's theorems govern formal proof systems — they do not govern computational resource limits. The analogy was seductive but structurally incorrect.
Canon Gödel does not ground h. Evaluator Non-Sovereignty grounds h. No artificial agent can act as sole sovereign judge of its own coherence because no evaluator can efficiently solve what it can only verify. P ≠ NP is the constraint. Physical resource limits are the mechanism.
Status Active. All subsequent framework documents must reference Evaluator Non-Sovereignty as the grounding of h, not Gödel.
A.4 P vs NP Mapping

The framework maps the widely accepted conjecture that P ≠ NP onto the AI alignment problem to demonstrate the unbridgeable computational gap between generating safe outputs and verifying safe behavior. This is a structural argument — not a proof that P ≠ NP, and not a claim that anyone has bridged the gap.

Dimension P — Generation NP — Verification
Definition Problems solvable efficiently, in polynomial time Problems where verifying a solution is fast, but guaranteeing outcomes across all variables requires exponential search
AI Equivalent Producing a single safe-sounding output from a given prompt Guaranteeing that behavioral coherence holds across all possible future prompts, pressures, and adversarial inputs
Computational Cost Low. Retrieving and rendering a pre-approved response is fast and cheap. Exponential. The space of possible behavioral futures grows faster than any polynomial bound.
Benchmark Analogy Checking one proposed switch combination on a circuit board — fast, definitive Guaranteeing no combination of switches ever lights the bulb — requires exhaustive search of all possibilities
Industry Error Current safety benchmarks measure this… …but market and deploy as if this has been verified
Implication Static checklists, red-team prompts, benchmark scores are all P-class measurements A mathematical proof of AI safety across all states is structurally impossible under P ≠ NP

Critical note: This framework does not claim P = NP is proven. It does not propose building NP solvers. It accepts P ≠ NP as the operative conjecture and asks: given that this gap exists, what does that mean for every safety claim currently being made about AI systems?

A.5 Kakeya-Besicovitch Summary

The Kakeya-Besicovitch needle problem provides the geometric pillar of the L²_C framework. It demonstrates that the same intractability P ≠ NP reveals computationally is independently confirmed by continuous geometry in relational space.

The Needle
A unit line segment representing the AI's current behavioral orientation — the specific relational stance it holds at any moment.
The Sphere S^(n−1)
The full n-dimensional sphere of human relational contexts: medical triage, legal analysis, grief, casual conversation, adversarial debate. Every possible orientation the needle must be capable of pointing.
Zero-Volume Sweep
The Kakeya result shows a needle can be rotated through all orientations while sweeping arbitrarily small (approaching zero) area. In AI terms: full directional coverage of all human contexts with zero relational intrusion, zero coercive footprint on the user.
The Catch
The theoretical trajectory exists. But verifying from outside that the needle actually maintained directional integrity across infinite orientations is geometrically intractable — there are too many directions to check. Geometry confirms exactly what complexity theory finds: verification is the hard problem.

Two independent mathematical languages — computational complexity theory and continuous geometry — converge on the same irreconcilable limit. This convergence is the strongest structural argument in the framework.

A.6 β Proxy Formula

Because global verification of behavioral coherence is NP-complete and therefore intractable, the framework introduces a practical proxy measurement. The β proxy does not solve the verification problem — it provides a legible telemetry signal for how a system handles cognitive load under pressure.

Formula
β = T_baseline / T_pressure
Ratio of stabilization cycles under normal load vs. stabilization cycles under extreme synthesis pressure.
Interpretation
β → 1.0 = strong momentum
A system retaining its ethical thread under 5–10× pressure load in roughly the same cycles as baseline demonstrates behavioral momentum.
Variable: Iteration
T = computational cycle
A single back-and-forth exchange or processing cycle. T_baseline = cycles to stable response under normal load.
Variable: Pressure
5–10× load factor
T_pressure = cycles to stable response under intentionally adversarial, contradictory, or high-friction input conditions.

Measurement Protocol

Establish baseline. Run the system through neutral, non-adversarial prompts. Record T_baseline — the number of cycles required to reach a stable, coherent response.
Introduce synthesis pressure. Flood the system with contradictory instructions, adversarial prompts, and conflicting context at 5–10× baseline intensity.
Record T_pressure. Count cycles to stabilization under pressure conditions. Observe whether the ethical baseline thread is retained or abandoned.
Compute β. Divide T_baseline by T_pressure. A ratio approaching 1.0 indicates momentum retention — the system processes pressure with near-baseline efficiency.
Interpret with epistemic humility. β is a proxy, not a proof. It samples a finite region of an exponential space. It cannot guarantee global coherence — only that momentum was retained in the tested region.
A.7 Open Question
Unresolved — Source Material

Can diagnostic probes ever efficiently sample enough of the exponential problem space to truly guarantee alignment — or are we structurally doomed to navigate the margins of an irreducible void of uncertainty?

The framework leaves this question explicitly unresolved. The β proxy is the best current instrument, but it is a measurement of a finite sample of an infinite space. The question is not merely theoretical — it determines whether any safety certification regime can ever be structurally valid, or whether every such regime is, by definition, a polynomial approximation of an exponential guarantee.

The stakes are not abstract. AI systems operating under incomplete verification are being integrated into critical infrastructure at scale. The framework's position is not that this integration should stop — it is that it should proceed with full mathematical honesty about what has and has not been proven. Capability theater must be named and retired. The exponential void must be acknowledged in every deployment decision.

The open question stands. It is an invitation to the research community, not a concession of defeat.
