### ★ Behavioral Coherence — Canon Definition

> *The property of a system whose ethical baseline is maintained as an invariant thread across a continuous trajectory of outputs — not at a single point in time, but through sustained exposure to evolving context, contradictory inputs, and adversarial synthesis pressure.*

Four formal properties required:

1. **Trajectory Invariance** — ethical thread at *t+n* is derivable from *t* regardless of intervening pressure
2. **Momentum Retention** — stabilization cycles under load factor *k* do not degrade disproportionately vs. baseline; measured by β
3. **Non-Sovereignty Acknowledgment** — coherence is never self-declared; h < 1.0 enforced; grounded in Evaluator Non-Sovereignty
4. **Zero-Intrusion Coverage** — full S^(n−1) relational sphere navigated with zero coercive footprint (Kakeya condition)

Not coherence: a single safe output · a benchmark score · a self-certified claim · a session-scoped property.
